# Chapter 823: Dao Monarch Bu, Your Strategy Was Invaluable

Zhang Yu stared at the giant corpse before him, the being Ying Xintian had identified as a former sect master of the Heavenly Demon Sect, but his mind remained clouded with confusion.

The information Ying Xintian had left behind, including the memories and dreams contained within the ancestors' corpses, never clearly explained the being's identity or its purpose.

All Zhang Yu could surmise was that this entity might have been Ying Xintian's collaborator and possibly the creator of the Berserk Corpse Components now plaguing the labyrinth.

Hearing the giant corpse's question, Zhang Yu replied calmly, "On this path of the Immortal Dao, who among us hasn't faced countless crises and overcome perilous obstacles? All we can do is persevere, no matter how many times we fall."

The giant corpse transmitted its thoughts to Zhang Yu privately, sighing, "Persevere... Well said. But if you could grasp more intelligence and better understand the workings of Kunxu, you could avoid many detours."

Zhang Yu's gaze flickered. He also transmitted his thoughts back, "Senior, what guidance do you have for me?"

The giant corpse replied, "Ying Xintian's legacy is more than just an inheritance; it's a network of connections. Are you aware of this?"

Seeing Zhang Yu nod, the giant corpse continued, "But an Immortal's network — an Immortal Network, you might call it — is not something an ordinary person can easily leverage."

"To truly utilize this Immortal Network and turn it into a force propelling you up the Immortal Path, you must first understand the current mindset of Kunxu's Immortals. Only then will you know how to use them."

Zhang Yu eyed the corpse and its True Spirit, which had been dead for who knew how many years, with a hint of skepticism. "Senior, you know all this?"

The giant corpse smiled faintly, unfazed by Zhang Yu's doubt. "Do you know why Immortals rarely intervene directly?"

Drawing on his understanding of Kunxu, Zhang Yu ventured, "Is it because Immortals... have more important, more profitable matters to attend to?"

"You're about half right," the giant corpse replied. "Immortals do chase profit, always directing their power toward the most lucrative endeavors. But that doesn't mean they've abandoned everything else."

"To grasp this, you must first understand what power and authority truly mean to an Immortal."

As Zhang Yu listened intently, the giant corpse slowly explained, "Power — whether it's one's Dao Lineage, Cultivation Realm, or specific Immortal Dao Technology — is the very foundation of an Immortal. It's the source of their confidence to reside high in the Nine Heavens and the eternal pursuit of all Immortals."

"But power is ultimately finite. Every bit used is a bit consumed. Even at the Immortal Realm — though they may have escaped the 'Three Debts' and are no longer bound by the 'Five Insurances,' free from karma and worldly leaks — they still face competition."

Hearing this, Zhang Yu sighed. "Even at the Immortal Realm, they still have to compete so fiercely?"

"Of course," the giant corpse said. "In fact, the competition becomes even more intense at the Immortal Realm. If you're an ordinary person, you can choose not to compete and just live out your days in obscurity until you die. But for an Immortal, to give up competing, to start falling behind, means facing the devouring ambitions of other Immortals. At that point, death would be a mercy."

"Finite power, coupled with endless competition, means that to stay ahead, Immortals must expend their limited power on... those areas that yield the highest personal profit."

"Wasting power on low-yield areas means falling behind, which means danger."

Zhang Yu mused aloud, "What if two Immortals expend their power in the same place, leading to a direct conflict?"

The giant corpse replied, "That's the last thing an Immortal wants to see. Both sides would deplete each other's power through combat, reducing their returns. If this happens repeatedly, both combatants could start lagging behind other Immortals."

Zhang Yu nodded thoughtfully. "Power... it's like money. Money is finite, and only by investing it in areas with the highest profit margins can you maintain a leading edge while everyone else's wealth is also growing."

"And at the Immortal Realm, everyone is likely highly skilled and rarely makes mistakes. They must all be utterly determined to climb the Immortal Path. So... every Immortal wastes very little power, and every Immortal strives to maintain rapid growth."

"This means... after becoming an Immortal, you can't afford to waste power. You must invest your limited power into high-growth projects to maintain your lead and avoid being targeted by other Immortals."

The giant corpse nodded and sent a mental message: "You've grasped that point. Now, let's discuss authority."

"Unlike power, authority... in a sense, can be infinite."

Zhang Yu frowned. "Infinite?"

The giant corpse explained, "You are currently a Rank 4 Sect Staff. If you lend your authority to your subordinate, allowing them to issue commands in your stead, haven't you essentially duplicated your authority?"

Zhang Yu nodded slightly. "Indeed."

Fu Ji nodded in agreement. "Indeed."

The giant corpse continued, "Bestowing and duplicating out of thin air, that is the nature of authority."

"An Immortal's power is finite, so it is all invested in the highest-growth, most crucial projects."

"But an Immortal's authority is nearly infinite. Through the systems they establish, they can command billions of living beings across the world to serve them."

"And authority is built upon power. Only by possessing immense power and maintaining its high growth can one sustain their authority. If power is excessively depleted in conflict, causing one to fall behind, their authority will gradually weaken as well."

Listening to the giant corpse's words, Zhang Yu seemed to have a sudden realization. "So... the Immortals' power is used for the most crucial matters... to profit themselves. Then they use their authority to influence other areas... to continue profiting themselves."

"This means that if I recklessly try to use the Immortal Network to summon an Immortal's power and have them use force to help me, it's something they would detest — unless the profit margin of the matter is absurdly high, surpassing what they're currently working on."

"But if... I use Ying Xintian's Immortal Network to grant myself authority, and in the process, generate profit for the Immortals, that would be an approved method. It's what the Immortals of Kunxu have been doing all along."

Recalling his past experiences, Zhang Yu said, "So... I need to become a pawn of the Immortal Network and let them use me."

The giant corpse nodded. "To do that, you must understand the Immortal Network Ying Xintian has guided you toward. You need to know what they're thinking and what they need."

"These former companions of Ying Xintian are dissatisfied with the current state of Kunxu. It is precisely because of their existence that many reforms have been implemented, such as the College Entrance Exam, the Foundation Establishment Certificate Exam, and the Sect Examination."

"This is partly due to the lingering ideals of the Righteous Path in their hearts, and partly to maintain Kunxu's vitality and sustain its overall growth."

"But they aren't as extreme as Ying Xintian. They don't want to sacrifice too much of their own interests in these reforms, lest they fall from their lofty positions."

Just as Zhang Yu nodded slightly, marveling at how much the giant corpse knew, it continued, "And your identity is very useful to them."

"From the First Layer of Kunxu, you used the Foundation Establishment Certificate Exam to enter one of the Ten Great Sects. You then advanced with fierce determination, passing the first Lower Realm Sect Examination to join a sect. You are the perfect banner for the success of these reforms."

Zhang Yu's heart jolted. "Senior, you even know all this?"

The giant corpse gave a mysterious smile and didn't answer, simply continuing, "Furthermore, you stood against Immortal Slayer and Fu Xiantian during the University War. This shows you've drawn a clear line between yourself and Ying Xintian, rejecting his ideals."

"These two points already make you usable — qualified to be a banner for reform, a pawn in their game."

"But merely being a pawn isn't enough to climb high or go far on the Immortal Path."

"If you want to climb higher and go farther in today's Kunxu, you must become a blade in the hands of the Immortals."

"And if you wish to ascend to the peak of this 36-layered Kunxu, you must first be unleashed as a blade, then successfully break free. That is the most difficult and highest immortal path."

"Are you willing?"

Zhang Yu nodded firmly. "Of course, I am."

The giant corpse said, "Then do you know that once you step onto this path, it often leads to certain death? You could fall into a danger that bankrupts you for ten, even a hundred lifetimes."

"But if you're willing to surrender the Immortal legacy now, you might still have a chance to live this life in peace."

"Your identity and your deeds are already enough to make you a piece on the board, but you must constantly reinforce this position. Make yourself the unwavering banner of reform."

"Your greatest value right now is, first, your own potential. But that still needs time to be realized and isn't ready to be deployed yet. Second is your current control over the labyrinth. This is crucial. You must familiarize yourself with it as quickly as possible..."

Zhang Yu was slightly startled. "Yun Taizhen?"

The Sect Examination Clone cried out anxiously, "That's an Immortal! We can't oppose an Immortal!"

The giant corpse explained, "In the Immortal Network Ying Xintian introduced you to, all have conflicts with Yun Taizhen — conflicts of interest and conflicts of Dao Lineage."

"As long as you are willing to point your blade at Yun Taizhen, the hilt will naturally be held by these Immortals; they will then become your backers."

The giant corpse sighed. "Displaying loyalty, showing determination — these are all good. But handing them the hilt, letting them control you, will satisfy them far more than any loyalty, determination, or ideology."

"This doesn't mean you have to fight Yun Taizhen to the death. Your target can be the Supreme Truth Immortal Clan under her. That, too, is opposing her."

"Conveniently, you also know Bu Yingshu of the Myriad Laws Immortal Clan. That's excellent... she can also be used against Taizhen..."

"You need to give the Immortals opportunities to exert their power, find angles for them to strike, and make it so they only need to lightly push the hilt to profit..."

Ever since he had acquired Ying Xintian's bloodline power from Ying Yunyao's corpse and upgraded Immortal Descendant to Level 6, he had felt a profound connection to the labyrinth.

"I'll be the one to control the labyrinth?"

Immortal Slayer said, "If production can be restored here in the labyrinth, its total output, economic value, and industry influence... will all be immense."

It's uncertain how much benefit we'll ultimately gain. Besides... our degree of control over this place is the key. The stronger our control, the greater our value, and the larger our share of the profits."

Just then, Zhang Yu's perception of the labyrinth entered a new stage.

In his senses, the entire labyrinth seemed to be enveloped in a hazy mist, growing denser the deeper he probed, repeatedly severing its connection to the Spirit Realm.

"Is this why the Spirit Realm connection was cut off?"

"Then..."
